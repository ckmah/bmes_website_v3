Links and Additional Resources

UC San Diego ISPE

The UC San Diego International Society of Pharmaceutical Engineers (ISPE) is another student organization under the Bioengineering department that is a great resource for bioengineering students. They offer resources more focused towards the pharmaceutical industry. It is primarily aimed at the Bioengineering: Biotechnology tract over the Bioengineering, Bioinformatics, and Biosystems tracts. While the events are less about building community among undergraduate students, the events are geared toward professional development, opportunities including resume workshops, information sessions, and career development workshops. BMES occasionally holds joint events with ISPE including workshops and industry panels.

Learn more

Bioengineering Graduate Society

We frequently collaborate with the Bioengineering Graduate Society (BEGS) at UC San Diego, through planning events for the Bioengineering community and through networking events that allow our members to mingle. Some of these include Bioengineering Day, a department wide event, as well as our Mentor-Mentee Program. The graduate students are great mentors and examples that our undergraduate students look up to. Learn about BEGS' initiatives and how they impact the Bioengineering community.

Learn more

Undergraduate Bioinformatics Club

The Undergraduate Bioinformatics Club (UBIC) caters specifically to the Bioinformatics community here at UC San Diego. They are a great resource for learning technical skills and industry opportunities through hands-on workshops and industry panels. Since the Bioinformatics major is offered in 4 different departments UBIC is a unique opportunity for these students to connect through UBIC's events. Learn more about the club as well specific opportunities they offer.

Learn more

Bioengineering Undergraduate Page

The official undergraduate site for Bioengineering at UC San Diego provides official information regarding the Bioengineering program. While the pages are somewhat unorganized, the information provided is accurate. Pages ranges from prospective students to senior design descriptions encompassing the whole range of undergraduate students. The latest curriculum updates and class offerings are usually announced here.
